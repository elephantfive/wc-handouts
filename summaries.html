Summaries

Summaries A summary is a condensed retelling of a text’s main idea(s) in your own words. How to Write a Summary The key to summarizing is to read the text as many times as necessary to understand it well. You cannot write a strong summary unless you understand the text. 1. Read, highlight, and annotate the text. Apply these strategies before, during, and after reading: 2. Create a thesis that includes the title of the text, author(s), and main idea. a. For example: The article “Global Epidemiology of Mental Disorders: What Are We Missing” by Baxter, Patton, Scott, Degenhardt, and Whitford (2013) looked at the mental health needs of different populations around the world and found that many areas did not have good data. 3. Include all the main ideas from the text. Do not add any ideas or opinions that were not presented in the original text. 4. Keep the original sequence. Present ideas in the same order as the author’s ideas. Before Reading • Identify what the text is mainly about based on the title. In other words, what is the topic? • Preview the structure and organization of the text. Are there headings, chapters, or other section divisions? During Reading • Highlight/underline the key ideas in the text. • Read the text multiple times. • Identify the key idea(s) of each paragraph or section of the text. After Reading • What is the overall idea the author shares about the topic? • Does the author make an argument or take a stance about the topic?
